Creating a compelling article for Medium requires a clear structure, especially when writing about technical topics such as WebRTC and video call app development. It's essential to strike a balance between comprehensive information and approachability, ensuring the material is accessible to your intended audience. Here's a suggested structure:

**1. Introduction**
Start by explaining the purpose of your article. Introduce WebRTC and the video call app you'll be creating. Engage the reader by explaining how this knowledge could benefit them.

**2. Background**
Dive a bit deeper into the concept of WebRTC, its history, and its current applications. Also, touch upon the technical components briefly without getting too deep into the details just yet.

**3. Understanding WebRTC**
In this section, discuss the core principles and components of WebRTC:

- Explain Peer-to-Peer communication
- Detail the components of WebRTC (getUserMedia, RTCPeerConnection, RTCDataChannel)
- Discuss the role of STUN/TURN servers in NAT traversal

**4. Importance of WebRTC**
Explain the reasons WebRTC is becoming popular and its unique advantages. Discuss its role in real-time communication.

**5. Creating a Video Call App Using WebRTC: Overview**
Outline the overall process and the technologies that you will use. This might include the programming languages, the role of HTML/CSS/JavaScript, and any frameworks or libraries you'll use.

**6. Setting Up Your Development Environment**
Briefly explain the environment setup needed for the project, including necessary software installations and configurations.

**7. Developing the Video Call App**
Here's where the meat of your article lies. Use subheadings for each step, and describe each part of the process in detail. Include plenty of code examples. Consider these steps:

- Designing the frontend (HTML/CSS)
- Implementing WebRTC on the client side (JavaScript)
- Setting up the server (if required)
- Implementing the signaling (WebSocket, etc.)
- Managing Session Descriptions
- Handling ICE Candidates
- Implementing Call/Answer functionality

**8. Testing and Debugging**
Explain how to test the application locally, troubleshoot common issues, and debug effectively.

**9. Deployment and Considerations**
Discuss how to deploy the app for public use. Also, cover some considerations like scalability, security, and performance.

**10. Conclusion**
Summarize the key points of your article. Encourage the reader to experiment with their new knowledge and provide avenues for further learning.

**11. References/Resources**
Provide a list of useful resources for those who want to learn more about WebRTC and related technologies.

Keep in mind that technical writing is about breaking complex topics into digestible pieces. Your goal should be to make this topic accessible and exciting to the reader, so use clear, concise language and plenty of examples. Also, remember to engage the reader and encourage them to put what they're learning into practice.
